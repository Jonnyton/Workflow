Terminology Style Guide
Cataclysm Terms
• Public/default POV: the Cataclysm


• Accepted public variants: the Time Between Ages, the Rebirth


• Restricted/informed usage only: multi-cycle framing, "last cataclysm"
History Terms
• Public: "Age of Bondage" as official inherited label


• Restricted: inversion framing (relative-equality era)
Royal Terms
• Public: Royals as elevated/divine-proximate


• Restricted: mortals with strong defensive advantages, not invincible
Writing Rule
Term choice is a POV signal. If uncertain, choose public terminology unless scene access explicitly justifies restricted terms.

Aurelith System
System Overview
The Aurelith System encompasses the planet Aurelith and its surrounding bodies, including key timeline events, influence networks, and interstellar routing context.
Key Facts
• Aurelith is the only habitable world in its system.


• The system includes multiple small bases (primarily scientific/technical outposts).


• Aurelith is a high-concentration node of elite capital, royal lines, and Web continuity infrastructure.


• The dominant continental logistics spine is a central-lake dual-drainage river corridor with major east/west trade-road pairing.
Moon City (Restricted Knowledge)
• A hidden moon city functions as the safe landing/wake hub for incoming upper-class travelers emerging from stasis.


• Dominant traffic is interstellar arrival, wake, negotiation, and planetary insertion.


• Negotiation is required because planetary power is pre-occupied by incumbent houses/institutions.


• Post-wake options include: choosing planetary nation alignment, returning to stasis, or chartering onward transit.


• The moon city is preferred by some upper-class families because it does not require low-tech social masking practiced on the planetary surface.


• Access works as a de facto wealth/secrecy gate through elite intermediaries, rather than a universal formal prohibition.


• In crisis politics, it can operate as elite temporary exile space for alliance rebuilding.
• Star class and full orbital architecture.


• Settlement chronology for moon city build phases.


• Dispatch/arrival ledger ties and governance protocols for moon hub access.
Seeded Worlds Influence Network
Cross-system influence ownership is maintained via durable institutional networks and long-horizon contracts.
Planets & Locations
Aurelith
The following details are hard constraints for any story set on Aurelith.
Canon Naming
• Planet/story reference: Aurelith


• Formal ruling state: The Crown Union of Aurelith


• Common speech uses the power-center nation name for the whole world.


• Aurelith sits at the western end of the great river corridor and functions as the key west-river maritime handoff zone.
Power Structure
• Public regime: noble houses under royal bloodline myth.


• Practical reality: oligarchic power games where commoners absorb the cost.


• Hidden continuity layer: the Web, a long-lived network embedded across nations and institutions.


• Deep secret: highest royal lines are off-world in origin (or descendants), hidden as "Royal Blood."


• Aurelith is a primary concentration point where capital elites, royal houses, and Web nodes overlap most strongly.


• Macro-order role: Aurelith functions as the planetary hegemon and coercive unifier; when its central order fractures, inter-polity war risk spikes globally through cascading power vacuums.
Society Model
• Public life reads as late-medieval frontier-to-feudal depending on region.


• Cultures are discipline traditions and inherited civic crafts, not simplistic species bins.


• Myth, ritual, and selective record-keeping are major governance tools.


• Continental life is organized around a central high-lake east/west river corridor and its paired trade roads.
Technology Visibility Model
1. Surface tech (common): hand tools, mills, smithing, river craft, signal fires, herbal medicine.


2. Institutional tech (rare): engineered hydraulics, precision lensing, coded archives, specialist harmonics.


3. Buried legacy tech (forbidden/rare): seed-ship relics, memory cores, field resonators, planetary failsafes.
Exposure Rules
• 80-90% of scenes must remain in surface-tech language and lived reality.


• If institutional/buried tech appears, locals should frame it through ritual, taboo, or contested doctrine.


• Advanced mechanisms must drive social consequences (law, class violence, myth fracture), not pure spectacle.
Faction Families
• Crown Union royal houses


• River Wardens / regional wardens


• Canopy Keepers and analogous ecological orders


• Veiled Circle operatives (hidden strata)


• Regional craft houses, tribunal courts, and faith-legal custodians
Narrative Requirements
• Resource/ecology pressure must shape politics.


• Propaganda of freedom vs material unfreedom should appear in lived detail.


• Choice architecture should pit loyalty, survival, and truth against each other.
Banned Drift Patterns
• Contemporary corporate/bureaucratic diction overload.


• Technocratic clean-resolution with no cultural friction.


• Generic ending formulas without specific cost and aftershock.
Aurelith Moon City
Common Myth Name
"Atlantis" (rumor-layer name)
Summary
A restricted-access lunar city serving as elite stasis-arrival, orientation, and redistribution hub for Aurelith-system continuity traffic.


It also functions as the local star-system trading floor for Royal-gated galactic control-share markets.
Locked Facts
• Knowledge of the city is upper-class restricted.


• It is the safest known local landing/wake point for incoming long-duration travelers.


• Travelers may: select planetary destination/nation, re-enter stasis, or arrange onward charter travel.


• Some upper-class lineages prefer permanent/primary residency here to avoid low-tech presentation constraints of planetary cities.


• Access is not primarily legal-prohibited; it functions as a secrecy-and-wealth gate via elite broker networks.
Access Model
• De facto model: access requires knowledge of the secret plus ability to secure a ticket (self-funded or sponsored).


• There is no single canonical posted fee threshold; price precision is less important than class exclusion dynamics.


• Core filter is informational and socioeconomic exclusion, not an explicit universal ban.


• Sponsorship channel is narrative-dependent (family, faction, institution, or private deal network) rather than a single fixed gatekeeper.
Typical Traffic Pattern
• Primary flow is interstellar arrivals waking from long-duration cryo, staging on Moon City, then negotiating planetary placement.


• Surface power is already occupied by entrenched houses and organizations; arrivals rarely have immediate uncontested placement.


• Negotiation inputs are stacked: portfolio influence (shares), lineage position, alliances, and coercive leverage.


• No universal weighting applies; final terms are context- and power-map-dependent.


• Moon staging is therefore a practical necessity, not a ceremonial step.
Strategic Use Cases
• Elite business transit and relocation.


• Long-horizon migration and inheritance positioning.


• Political fallback/exile for weakened rulers rebuilding alliances before re-entry elsewhere.


• Conditional re-entry to cryo until specified time/events.


• Timed wake deployment of specialist cohorts (constitutional brokers, market operators, and occasional high-prestige military leadership) for crisis-window interventions.
Frequent Negotiation Trap
• Newcomers who liquidate too much of their share base to buy immediate court placement often gain status optics but lose autonomous power, becoming dependent on host-lord success and favor.
Surface Insertion Protocol
• Arrivals commonly legitimize through lineage records that are either authentic, negotiated, or forged.


• Some entrants are truly related to existing noble lines and are integrated via kinship claims.


• Others negotiate with powerful houses to establish politically recognized lineage narratives.
Ryn's Village (Ashwater Settlement)
Summary
A small frontier settlement on the near bank of the Ashwater river, where the river bends south. Ryn's home community during the opening of Book I.
Locked Facts
• Located in a valley accessed by descending a ridge trail from the north.


• Situated on the Ashwater's near bank at a southward bend.


• Low buildings with bark roofs, hunched against the earth. Enclosed by a timber palisade.


• Small enough that a missing hunter is felt, but large enough for specialised roles (farrier, healer).


• Hard winter conditions at Book I opening: herds moved south early, river froze before the last fish runs. Community under food pressure.


• The healer Maren resides here.
Canon Notes
• Formal settlement name not yet established; referred to by proximity to the Ashwater.


• Reads as surface-tech frontier community consistent with Aurelith's society model.

Factions
Web Influence Exchange Doctrine
Core Economic Model
The Web is best modeled as a galactic influence exchange. Shares represent durable influence rights over institutions, access channels, and elite reception pathways on seeded worlds.
Trading Access Constraint
• Direct galactic-share execution is Royal-gated and mediated through nanite-cloth + embedded agent-AI interfaces.


• Non-royal actors generally participate indirectly through delegated structures, layered ownership vehicles, or political influence over Royal executors.
Buyer Logic
Primary buyers are rich elites and elite organizations distributed across seeded systems. They buy influence long before possible physical arrival (often 1,000+ years later).
Why Shares Have Value
• They increase odds of elite-level landing conditions on arrival.


• They can be transferred/sold to finance relocation influence elsewhere.


• They are expected to survive local regime churn, collapse cycles, and restructurings.
Local Operator Incentives
Upper-tier secret society members are shareholders. Their mission is to preserve and increase long-horizon share value by maintaining continuity of control networks.
Continuity via Restructuring
Influence vehicles can merge, split, or be acquired (company succession model). Claims are not necessarily identical after restructuring, but continuity of value is marketed as a core promise.
Time-Horizon Pricing
Short local shocks (depressions, coups) often weakly affect price because signals and travel are slow. True revaluation events are long-term disruptions to Web control continuity.
Volatility Reality
Attack events can produce sharp fear repricing before real control-loss is measurable. Because covert survivorship is common, apparent collapses can later mean-revert, creating high-risk/high-reward contrarian windows.
Power Structure Reality
• The Web is not monolithic.


• Members cooperate because aligned incentives raise asset value.


• High-share blocs can resist orders from other blocs.


• Individuals are oath-bound publicly, ambition-driven privately.


• Historical note: elite network factions were willing to tolerate or accelerate extreme conflict conditions when systemic reset outcomes favored long-horizon hierarchy objectives.


• Alignment-era enforcement was existential: refusal to align could result in lineage-level elimination.


• Web control over affiliated institutions is uneven; some outwardly aligned blocs contain covert counter-line factions running compliance theater.


• Hardline zealot cadres exist whose loyalty ethic permits self-sacrifice and intimate-bond violence in service of mission continuity.


• Upper-rank cells can run controlled-provocation operations using compartmentalized agents and restricted predictive AI tooling unavailable to most members.


• Most Web operators are not aware of such plans and are often tasked with conventional stability/anti-rebellion objectives.
Institutional Control Vectors
Different blocs may dominate different mass institutions (e.g., church networks, trade guilds, legal channels, military patronage). Cross-institution bargaining drives planetary politics.
Density and Labor Strategy
• Web growth frequently proceeds through infiltration/funding/founding of firms and civic organizations in dense trade corridors.


• Labor-pool overlap creates trust propagation and recruitable surplus populations.


• Sparse regions are lower-yield control environments unless they hold chokepoint value.
Training Culture
• The Web-developed strategy game "Hex" is used as initiate conditioning for uncertainty management, market logic, and network-control reasoning.


• Advanced doctrine emphasizes shifting alliances, negotiated access pacts, and coordinated betrayal timing as core diplomatic competencies.
Veiled Circle
Summary
The Veiled Circle is a hidden continuity operator layer on Aurelith that manages long-horizon influence stability across regime changes.
Role
• Maintains institutional continuity and influence value over centuries.


• Shapes outcomes through proxy institutions (faith, guilds, courts, logistics).


• Avoids overt control signatures when possible.
Internal Reality
• Not monolithic; multiple blocs with competing methods.


• Coordination emerges from aligned share incentives more than trust.


• Power tracks with coalition strength and influence position, not just titles.
Relationship to Public Power
• Public story: royals and noble houses rule.


• Hidden practice: Circle-linked networks condition what remains politically possible.


• Even royals may operate under fear constraints relative to deeper secret-society enforcement power.
Crown Union of Aurelith
Formal ruling-state layer presenting public sovereignty while deeper continuity layers shape long-horizon outcomes.
Lineage Admission Mechanics
• Verified kin records can open negotiations but do not compel house acceptance.


• Origin families may politically reject even blood-proven entrants (e.g., black-sheep exclusion).


• Rejected entrants may found a new house or be adopted into alternate house alliances.


• Where kin records are absent or weak, lineage may be politically manufactured through inter-house bargaining.


• Record acceptance is as much power negotiation as archival truth; local power blocs decide final recognition.
High Church of the Blood
Largest Royalist church institution, organized around papal authority and bloodline-centered theological governance.
Canon Role
• One of the top three institutions required for long-horizon regime stability.


• Influences legitimacy, moral law, social obedience, and elite reputational status.


• Core doctrine elevates Royals-of-the-blood (off-world lineages and descendants) as near-divine authority.


• Fanatic currents include direct royal veneration.


• Contains covert influence penetration by Veiled Circle and other secret networks, including upper clerical ranks.


• Official supreme title is Pope.


• Teaches the grand liberation narrative: Royals defeated oppressive god-rulers at the Cataclysm and delivered peace/prosperity/freedom.


• Newest canon framing: this narrative is strategically selective and omits elite war-escalation and reset politics tied to post-cataclysm hierarchy entrenchment.


• Functions as the primary authority for official history, setting the narrative baseline most populations are taught.


• Benefits from (and often sustains) mass belief that Royals are unkillable, especially in strongly Church-governed regions.


• Applies class-sensitive narrative control: low-status contradiction is discredited/noised; high-status (especially Royal) contradiction is treated as strategic threat requiring rapid containment.
Geopolitical Function
• Highest clerical office (Pope) pushes peace frameworks between nations where royal-blood faith populations are large.


• Charity infrastructure (shelters/meal systems) builds public legitimacy while also concentrating vulnerable populations into controllable institutional zones.
Hierarchy Notes
• Cardinal layer functions as senior doctrinal-political authority.


• A black-pope / Jesuit-style inner-order structure exists as strategic backbone behind the public hierarchy.


• Common honorifics for the Pope include: His Holiness, Royal-Touched, and Your Excellency.Monastery Library Network


Distributed monastic archive system spanning the world, preserving and filtering strategic knowledge.
Canon Role
• One of the top three institutions required for durable power.


• Controls memory continuity, record legitimacy, and access to high-value texts.


• Serves as both scholarship layer and political instrument.


• Infiltrated by secret-society agents competing over archival control and narrative authority.


• Operationally aligned with Church official-history doctrine to maintain social narrative coherence.


• Reinforces anti-hoarding morality norms that centralize book custody and increase editorial control.Grand Merchant Naval Guild


Dominant merchant guild with the largest navy and majority control over global ocean trade routes.
Canon Role
• One of the top three institutions required for decade-plus political durability.


• Controls maritime logistics, trade taxation leverage, and naval coercive capacity.


• Functions as economic lifeline and strategic chokepoint authority.


• Subject to secret-society penetration and internal bloc competition.


• Regionally unchallenged in its principal ocean zone, with dominant north/south coastal trade lanes.
Secrecy Doctrine
• Publicly frames westward, far-north, and far-south routes as effectively impossible.


• Privately preserves scarce route-enabling capabilities (icebreakers, anti-scurvy knowledge, specialized storm vessels).


• Maintains scarcity to protect monopoly margins and strategic dependence.


• Faces eastern naval counterbalance coalitions that constrain expansion.


• Supports selective river-restoration narratives where they increase long-run logistics centralization under guild-friendly control.
Internal Social Structure
• Membership composition skews toward nobles with relatively fewer royals.


• Ship crews can hold equity in their vessels, creating more distributed ownership than many terrestrial power systems.


• This structure enables both hierarchy-preserving blocs and covert egalitarian-revision blocs to coexist.Tidemark Communion


Powerful merchant-elite faith that is adjacent to, but doctrinally weaker on, full Royalist blood deference.
Distinction
• Treats bloodline elites primarily as privileged ruling class rather than strict divine hierarchy.


• Strongly aligned to trade stability, maritime prosperity, and elite mercantile autonomy.


• Evangelizes river-restoration destiny: restoring full navigability is framed as peace/prosperity mission.
Hidden Program
• Restoration rhetoric also supports long-horizon logistics capture.


• Full river-to-ocean integration would shift inland flows into maritime-controlled chokepoints and strengthen Web-aligned control outcomes.


• Internal schism note: some leadership currents treat this not as pure Web consolidation but as a pathway to re-expand broad prosperity/equity under tighter anti-oligarchic constraints.
Internal Doctrine Split
• Public survival doctrine preserves hierarchy language (nobles above commoners, royals above nobles).


• Restricted inner belief (for some factions): wealth can exist, but nobility/royalty should not constitute quasi-separate social species.


• Double-agent dynamics are active due to perceived deep Web infiltration.
Political Position
• Tolerated by the Pope and High Church largely due to institutional power.


• Papal preference remains stronger deference to bloodline theology than Tidemark typically grants.River Wardens


Regional logistics/governance force connected to trade flow, river control, and enforcement politics.
Canopy Keepers
Ecological-order faction tied to forest stewardship, ritual authority, and territorial resistance.
Aurelith Expansion Inc

Canon Notes

Covenant of Living Lineage
Major Royalist branch emphasizing lawful stewardship and negotiated legitimacy over direct worship excess.
Distinction
• Treats bloodline as sacred duty, not unrestricted divine entitlement.


• Tends to mediate disputes and preserve inter-house compacts.
Ember Throne Revival
Hardline Royalist branch framing bloodline authority as militant sacred mandate.
Distinction
• Supports aggressive enforcement and purgative reform rhetoric.


• Elevates obedience and sacrificial loyalty to blood hierarchy.
Quiet Anointing
Esoteric Royalist branch focused on hidden duty, coded doctrine, and disciplined succession curation.
Distinction
• Prioritizes covert stewardship over public spectacle.


• Strong overlap with archive culture and inner-order networks.
Characters
Ryn
Summary
Ryn is a mixed-heritage Aurelith figure whose rebellion targets visible rulers while gradually uncovering deeper Web-linked control layers.
Book I Opening Characterization (Chapter 1)
• Lives in a frontier village on the Ashwater river; functions as a hunter providing for his community.


• Bonded with a female wolf companion — found her as a pup in a frozen den, small enough to carry in one hand. She serves as hunting partner, travois hauler, and emotional anchor.


• Skilled wilderness survivor: tracks, reads violence scenes, forages medicinal plants (pine resin, usnea lichen, birch bark), builds field poultices, constructs a travois, and navigates deep-winter timber.


• Father taught him foraging, tracking, and reading the land ("by walking and pointing and expecting Ryn to remember"). Mother died when Ryn was six or seven; she knew plants, and Ryn carries body-memory of her teaching.


• Personality in opening: dry, practical, selfless to the point of self-destruction. Prioritises a stranger's survival over his own exhaustion and the village's food shortage. Minimal self-pity.


• Physical state at chapter end: four days on bad food and no sleep, collapses within sight of the village after hauling Daeren's travois through a full day of ridge country in worsening snow.

• After collapse from exhaustion, a farm horse reaches him and carries him toward safety.


• A farmer family shelters and nurses him through near-fatal condition.

Post-Interlude Vow and Retaliation
• Vow: no civilians will die for hiding him again.


• He intentionally re-exposes himself by killing the captain tied to the farm burning, creating a visible incident to pull pursuit away from villagers.

• Veyr belongs to an older continuity stratum that has persisted through repeated civilizational breakdown-and-rebuild cycles.


• His practical lifespan is created by cryosleep segmentation: short wake interventions separated by very long dormant intervals.
Why Dormant by Default
• In peacetime, Veyr’s command legitimacy can overpower factional balance and threaten incumbent political bargains.


• His deterrence profile is too centralizing for routine elite competition.


• Keeping him asleep preserves elite maneuvering space while retaining him as emergency leverage.
Activation Logic (Compartmented)
• The objective is not chaos for its own sake.


• The objective is controlled instability sufficient to unlock emergency command and constitutional restructuring pathways.


• Once thresholds are met, Veyr’s return reframes repression as restoration and converts rebellion shock into governance centralization.
Post-Cataclysm Wake Pattern (Abstract)
• Veyr has already been awakened multiple times since the latest Cataclysm.


• Each wake follows a recognizable sequence: legitimacy fracture -> emergency mandate -> coercive stabilization -> constitutional re-lock -> re-entry to cryo.


• His deployments are deliberately finite; he is not permitted to remain continuously active long enough to permanently rewrite the cycle machinery.
Core Torment (Character Engine)
• Veyr understands that his interventions restore order but do not break the cataclysm cycle.


• He repeatedly saves civilizations only to be returned to cryo while structural drivers of future collapse remain intact.


• This creates his defining psychological wound: he is treated as history’s repair mechanism, never its cure.
Story Use Rule
Write Veyr as a tragic continuity weapon, not a simple tyrant or savior:


• brutally effective in restoration phases,


• existentially aware of cyclical failure,


• increasingly conflicted about serving systems that require recurring collapse to justify his return.

Age of Bondage
So-called pre-cataclysm era that official doctrine labels as bondage, but which was in reality comparatively more equal and prosperous than later royal-order society.
Canon Baseline
• Broad populations had stronger awareness of shared off-world origin and lived with lower class stratification than post-cataclysm order.


• The term "Age of Bondage" is a political inversion label used to create contrast for later royal-legitimacy narratives.

A civilization-defining turning point referenced by nearly all power blocs, with heavily contested interpretation and perspective-dependent naming.
Terminology Discipline
• Public/default usage in most stories and characters: the Cataclysm (singular).


• Accepted public variants: the Time Between Ages, the Rebirth.


• "Last Cataclysm" / multi-cycle framing is restricted to rare informed circles.
Canon Baseline
• In public narrative, the Cataclysm follows the so-called Age of Bondage.


• Escalating wars among elite/secret-society-aligned blocs were intentionally intensified toward cataclysmic reset conditions.


• A pre-collapse market regime shift preceded open conflict: minority blocs quietly accumulated low-valued 'dead' societies, rebuilt alliance architecture, and forced large-scale repricing.


• Cataclysm outcomes included mass population loss, rival elimination, and structural opportunity to impose stronger royal hierarchy.


• In restricted-history context, this event is one entry in a longer cycle sequence.
Contested Narrative Layer

Core Theme
History is lived through asymmetric perspective: different classes and factions hold different fragments, and behavior follows what each believes to be true.

• The Church of the Blood defines and broadcasts official history.

• Most of the population accepts this narrative as truth because it is socially authoritative and institutionally universal.

Key Mechanics
• Most units move 1 hex per turn.


• Scouts can perform expanded recon from advantageous terrain (e.g., hills).


• Organization profitability rises with network synergies (complementary city presence).


• City market entry can be exclusive unless existing players negotiate admission.


• Negotiated city-entry terms can be arbitrarily complex, turning diplomacy into a primary strategic weapon.


• Buyouts can remove rivals from non-village positions.


• Units (scouts/soldiers) are expensive and carry ongoing maintenance costs.


• Table stakes are variable; entering with large capital/token inventory is called coming in heavy.


• Many rule sets enforce minimum buy-ins so every player carries meaningful downside.
Token Classes
• Generic organization tokens represent baseline ventures.


• Specialized tokens (official, sealed, precious-material pieces) represent enhanced ventures (e.g., ironwood-grade logging) with stronger terrain-matched profits.
Circulation Mechanic
Money spent by players enters a shared pool and is redistributed on following turns by organizational profitability, making spending intensity a core driver of economic velocity.
Win/Loss Model
• Lose: run out of money.


• Win: remain last actor controlling the system (“owning it all”).
Origin and Institutional Use
Hex was originally developed within Web circles and later diffused into broader culture.


The Web uses structured Hex play as part of initiate training.
Cultural Function
Hex acts as entertainment and as training in fog-of-war reasoning, trade-route logic, monopoly formation, dealmaking, and economic warfare.


At advanced levels, its decisive layer is diplomatic engineering: alliance choreography, term-structured access, and timed betrayal.
Hex Token Economy
Official Components
• High-stakes Hex uses officially sealed specialized tokens minted from precious materials.


• Specialized tokens can grant superior return profiles under matching terrain/resource conditions.
Capital Entry Model
• Players choose starting stake (money + token inventory).


• Entering with large starting capital/token stack is known as coming in heavy.


• Many tables enforce minimum buy-in to ensure meaningful risk participation.
Money Circulation Rule
• Spent money moves into a common pool.


• On the next turn, pool value redistributes across companies by profitability weighting.


• This creates dynamic money velocity tied to active spending and organizational expansion.
Class Variants
• Lower-income tables often run low-stake rules and restrict/omit specialized tokens.


• Noble/Royal tables often permit unrestricted heavy entry and broader token classes.

Canon Sequence
1. Wolf death + severe wounds + continuous pursuit.


2. Delirium/exhaustion collapse in farming hinterland.


3. Horse-assisted retrieval; farmer family concealment and care.


4. Short period of quiet labor/recovery.


5. Soldier reprisal sweep; farm burned, family killed, village pressured.


6. Ryn internalizes event as personal culpability.


7. Ryn vows no one will die hiding him again.


8. He kills the captain linked to the farm burning and deliberately draws pursuit away from villagers.


9. He extends pursuit long enough to relocate the active search radius deep into wilderness away from villages.


10. He begins attritional guerrilla elimination once civilian zones are out of scope.


11. He leaves one survivor to carry explicit warning details.


12. Terrified command personnel suppress the truth and stage a false death confirmation with a prisoner double.


13. Nobles accept the decayed trophy and consider Ryn dead.


14. He withdraws from civilization afterward.
Narrative Function
This event is Ryn’s first post-collapse trust attempt and the bridge from helpless flight to guilt-forged, civilian-protective predation strategy, followed by a state-level false-death myth that reshapes his threat profile.

After the forest pursuit collapse, surviving command elements fabricated proof of Ryn’s death to avoid further deployments against him.
Canon Sequence

Aurelith River Pressure Campaign
Summary
Aurelith expands border control along river corridors through phased coercion designed to prevent inter-village unity and force piecemeal annexation.
Core Sequence ("Carrot -> Stick")
1. Offer phase: local garrisons present bilateral village-level deals (security, trade access, reduced immediate violence).


2. Fragmentation phase: villages are encouraged to negotiate separately, not collectively.


3. Instability phase: covert saboteurs generate local disorder (raids, shortages, accusations, infrastructure incidents).


4. Rescue phase: garrison re-offers aid tied to accession terms.


5. Threat phase: if refused again, garrison warns of siege/escalation.


6. Forced-choice phase: delayed local leadership arrives at worse terms under pressure.
Why It Works
• Villages believe joint resistance triggers overwhelming main-army response.


• Secret-society actors inside villages spread fear and promote compliance narratives.


• Leadership splits and delay windows erode bargaining power before open assault is needed.
Social Effect
• Populations become polarized: many demand independence and resistance, while elites and risk-averse factions push transactional submission.


• Even resisting villages are often isolated and negotiated down one by one.
Story Use

Royal Power Profile
Core Pattern
Royals possess broad access to culture-linked abilities without formal apprenticeship, but usually lack the depth/mastery of lineage-trained specialists.
Mortality Clarification
• Royals are not truly invincible and can be killed.


• Healing benefits are real but limited, and can be outperformed by some specialist non-royal practitioners.


• Practical survivability is heavily boosted by autonomous nanite-defense response.
Social Usage Norm
• Core discipline powers are often treated as "commoner-level" in elite etiquette.


• Royals typically reserve direct use for suppression, counterforce, or symbolic dominance displays.
Signature Royal Expression
• Royal garments are nanite systems capable of morphing by intent into ceremonial regalia, armor, and weapons.


• This visible transformation is usually sufficient on its own to establish Royal status in public perception.


• The social boundary between rich noble and Royal is primarily this unmistakable power-legibility.


• While covert concealment is possible, most Royals avoid it due to pride and risk aversion.
Covert Concealment Method
• Primary method is restraint: do not activate nanite-regalia signatures in public.


• Effective blending requires performance of local skill norms and cultural behavior to avoid elite-pattern tells.
Common Cover-Break Triggers
• Forced reveal under survival pressure (especially while losing a fight).


• Injury states that make restraint untenable.


• Ego/dominance reveal behavior (e.g., taunt before kill when witness risk is judged controllable).


• Intentional reveal when mission objective is complete.


• Narrative caveat: trigger mix varies by individual and situation.
Immediate Reaction Matrix After Exposure
• Fear response is common, especially from those who previously treated the Royal as socially inferior or intimate subordinate.


• Worship response is common among strong Royalist-faith adherents.


• Bargaining/coordination response is common in selective/private reveal contexts where survival and advantage can still be negotiated.


• Exact distribution is relationship-dependent, audience-dependent, and situation-dependent.
Political-Economic Constraint
• Royal superiority in symbolic force and gated strategic systems does not equal total autonomy.


• Royals remain operationally dependent on noble-controlled real-economy infrastructure (ships, estates, firms, labor webs, local military financing) for sustained campaign execution.
