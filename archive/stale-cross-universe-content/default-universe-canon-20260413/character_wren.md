# Wren — The Stranger

## Identity

The woman who climbed the south face at dusk, coat cut in lowland style, pack riding crooked, carrying something wrapped in dark cloth held close enough that her exhaustion had shaped itself around protecting it. She gave the name **Wren** after three days of fever. Whether it is her real name remains an open question — she offered it the way someone offers a coin they can afford to lose.

She claims to be a cartographer from **Thessmark**. The cover collapses under pressure: her accent places her nowhere recognizable, her vowels flattened in a way that doesn't match Thessmark, river settlements, or highland Arrish dialect. She is educated in non-frontier ways, knows plant names Corin has never heard, and catalogs local customs with the precision of someone documenting rather than living.

## Physical Description

- **Age**: Appears early thirties, though something is off — a dissonance between the weathering of her face and the steadiness of her hands
- **Build**: Tall, gaunt in a way that predates her current condition — long-term lean, not recently starved. Narrow-shouldered, long-limbed
- **Skin**: Dark-skinned, hands calloused along the finger pads and outer palms — consistent with rope handling, climbing, or sustained tool use unfamiliar to the frontier
- **Scars**: A lattice of older scars across both forearms, too regular for accident, too shallow for torture. Possibly ritual, possibly occupational. A deep scar bisects her left eyebrow and continues across her temple
- **Hair**: Dark, cut short and unevenly by her own hand
- **Scent**: A mineral, bitter undertone — faint as a struck nail. Beneath the trail dirt and fever sweat, something chemical or geological clings to her

## Condition on Arrival

Half-dead when Corin found her on the switchback above the snowline. Hypothermic, dehydrated, badly swollen ankle, wound on her left side partially self-treated with an unfamiliar poultice made from herbs that don't grow in the Reach. Frostbite on three fingers of her right hand. She carried no pack, no provisions, no weapon, no water, no fire kit, no coin — only a folding knife with a bone handle and common steel, and a cured leather strip on her left wrist bearing faint markings in unknown script. And the cylinder, strapped beneath her ribs.

She said: *"I need help."* And when asked about the bundle: *"Nothing that will hurt you."*

She expected the wolf's refusal. *"I know,"* she said — and it landed harder than it should have.

## Behavior and Voice

Habitually quiet in a way that reads as nature rather than strategy. Answers direct questions with short factual responses and stops talking at sensitive topics without deflecting or deflecting badly. Watches everything with steady, cataloguing attention. Courteous in a spare way that suggests upbringing rather than performance.

When unguarded — usually during fever — her voice is warm and measured. When cornered, she becomes evasive in a specific pattern: she doesn't lie, she redirects. She slips into archaic or foreign syntax under stress, pausing mid-sentence as if translating from a language she thinks in but doesn't speak aloud.

**Key tell**: When conscious enough to speak, she asked what *season* it was. Not where she was.

## What She Carries

The **sealed leather cylinder** — roughly fourteen inches long, three inches in diameter. Dark material that looks like metal but isn't. No chill in freezing air, no condensation, weighs less than its density suggests, faintly warm to the touch like stone that sat in afternoon sun hours ago. It contains inscribed plates she can partially read, halting and backtracking, mouthing the shapes of Substrate Notation — a script the rest of Aurelith treats as sacred decoration on Votive Stones.

She checked it before she checked her wound. It is more vital to her than healing.

## What Wren Knows

She has been in the **Deep Interior** — the unmapped heart of Aurelith where the land's geometry stops pretending to be natural. She saw something there that broke her understanding of the world. She can partially read inscriptions that no living tradition teaches. In fever she has mentioned *coordinates*, *warnings*, *the listening layer*, and *root-level access* — terms that mean nothing in frontier vocabulary.

She recognized the description of the **grey soldiers** instantly. Not surprise — recognition. Her hand went to the cylinder beneath her coat.

She sees a Votive Stone without reaction. She already understands what they are.

## What Wren Doesn't Know

Why the land responded to her presence in the Interior. What the cylinder's contents ultimately describe. Whether her fever is illness or something the land is doing to her. Whether the people who sent the grey soldiers want the cylinder, want her, or want to ensure that whatever she found stays buried.

## Narrative Role

Wren is the catalyst — the stranger whose arrival converts Corin's grief-haunted subsistence into a journey. She is the first evidence that Corin's father Alden didn't vanish into madness but into something real. She carries proof that Aurelith's deepest structures were built, not born — but she carries it in a form she can only half-read, which means the answers she offers always generate more dangerous questions.

She is not a mentor, not a guide, not a love interest by default. She is a woman running from a recognition she cannot name, who landed in the one village where someone might understand why.

## Faction Connections

Deliberately ambiguous. Her ability to read Substrate Notation connects her to the **Reclaimers** (custodians of buried technology) or the **Ash Veil** (the loose network of people who've seen too much in the Interior) — or something older than either. The grey soldiers may serve the **Compact**, whose surveyors are already cataloguing Votive Stones with instruments that could detect the cylinder. The **Votary** would classify it as a sacred relic and her literacy as heresy.

She has not named any faction. She has not confirmed any allegiance. The cylinder and the scars tell a story she is not yet willing to speak aloud.
