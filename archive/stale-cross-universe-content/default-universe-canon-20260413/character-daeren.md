# Daeren

## Overview

A man found half-dead in the upper Vael Reach, carried into shelter on a makeshift travois. No surname offered, no settlement claimed. His injuries and exposure suggest days alone in the high country — longer than most survive without shelter or provisions. He arrived in the story as a body to be saved, not a person to be known, and that sequence matters: whoever Daeren is, the first thing established about him is that someone chose to drag him out of the cold.

## Physical Description

- **Age:** Difficult to judge beneath the damage. Somewhere between thirty and forty-five. Gaunt in a way that predates the exposure — this is a man who has been lean for a long time, not someone recently starved.
- **Build:** Tall, narrow-shouldered. Long limbs. Hands are calloused but not in the patterns of field labor or forge work — the wear sits along the finger pads and outer palms, consistent with rope handling, climbing, or sustained tool use of a kind the Reach doesn't commonly see.
- **Distinguishing marks:** A lattice of older scars across both forearms, too regular to be accidental, too shallow to be torture. Possibly ritual, possibly occupational. A single deep scar bisects his left eyebrow and continues faintly across the temple. His hair is dark, cut short and unevenly — done by his own hand, recently.
- **Condition on arrival:** Hypothermic, dehydrated, one ankle badly swollen. A wound on his left side, partially self-treated with a poultice of unfamiliar composition — herb knowledge from outside the Reach. Frostbite on three fingers of his right hand. Unconscious or near-unconscious when found.

## What He Carried

Almost nothing. No pack, no provisions, no weapon. What he had:

- A folding knife with a bone handle, the blade worn thin from years of resharpening. Common steel, not Compact-issue.
- A strip of cured leather wrapped around his left wrist, underneath the sleeve. Faint markings pressed into the inner surface — not Shaper-script, not any trade alphabet used in the Reach or the lowlands. Too small and worn to read clearly.
- Nothing else. No water skin, no fire kit, no coin. Either he was robbed, or he fled with only what was on his body.

## Behavior and Manner

When Daeren regains consciousness, he is quiet in a way that reads as habitual rather than guarded. He answers direct questions with short, factual responses. He does not volunteer information, but he does not deflect with obvious lies — he simply stops talking when a line of inquiry reaches something he won't discuss. There is no performance in it. He has the manner of someone who has spent long stretches alone and has lost the social reflex of filling silence.

He watches everything. Not with suspicion — with the steady, cataloguing attention of someone accustomed to reading unfamiliar environments. He notices the arrangement of a room, the condition of tools, the way people move around each other. He is courteous in a spare, unornamented way that suggests upbringing rather than strategy.

He does not ask where he is. He asks what season it is.

## What Is Known

- He came from the direction of the high passes — deeper country, not the lowland trade roads.
- His poultice herbs are not native to the Reach. Someone with knowledge of interior or southern flora taught him, or he learned it there himself.
- He is not Arrish. He is not Compact. He does not carry himself like military, guild, or clergy.
- His accent, when he speaks enough to reveal one, places him nowhere recognizable. The vowels are flattened in a way that doesn't match Thessmark, the river settlements, or the highland Arrish dialect.
- He knows what the Votive Stones are. When he sees one, his expression does not change — but he does not ask about it, which means he already understands what he is looking at.

## What Is Not Known

Everything that matters. Where he came from. Why he was alone in the high country without gear. What the scars on his arms signify. What the leather strip says. Why he asks about the season instead of the place. What he was doing in the deep interior — or beyond it — and what drove him out.

These are not questions the narrative should rush to answer. Daeren's value in the early story is as a human being in need of help, not as a source of exposition. The mystery earns its weight by remaining embodied — carried in the scars, the missing gear, the herbs that don't grow here, the quiet — rather than narrated.

## Narrative Role

Daeren is a catalyst whose presence should feel incidental before it feels significant. He is rescued because the protagonist is the kind of person who rescues strangers, not because the plot requires a mysterious arrival. The questions he raises should accumulate through observed detail, not interrogation. He is not a mentor, not a villain, not a guide — he is a man who survived something, and whatever that something was connects to the deeper architecture of Aurelith in ways the early story is not yet ready to name.

## Relationships

- **The protagonist:** Owes a debt. Daeren is not comfortable with debts. This tension — between genuine gratitude and the discomfort of obligation — should be legible in small behaviors, not stated aloud.
- **Ashka / the wolf:** The wolf's reaction to Daeren matters. Whether the animal is calm, wary, or unusually attentive around him is a signal the narrative can plant early and pay off later.
- **The settlement:** A stranger in the upper Reach after the grey soldier raids will not be welcomed easily. Daeren's presence tests the community's capacity for trust at a moment when trust is already strained.
